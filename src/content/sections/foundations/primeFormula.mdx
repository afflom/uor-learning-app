  return (
    <SubsectionTemplate metadata={primeFormula}>
      <p>
        Let \(D\) be a Unique Factorization Domain (UFD) with group of units \(U(D)\).
        Denote by \(P\) the set of equivalence classes of irreducible elements (primes)
        of \(D\) modulo units. For each nonzero \(x\in D\), define the
        <strong>valuation</strong> at a prime \(p\in P\) by:
      </p>
      <p style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
        \[v_p(x) = \max\{n\in\mathbb{N}: p^n \mid x\},\]
      </p>
      <p>
        By the UFD property, \(v_p(x)\) is finite and zero for all but finitely
        many \(p\in P\). The <strong>Prime Formula</strong> states the unique
        decomposition
      </p>
      <p style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
        \[x = u\cdot \prod_{p\in P} p^{v_p(x)},\]
      </p>
      <p>
        where \(u\in U(D)\) is the unique unit making the equality hold,
        and the infinite product is effectively finite due to vanishing valuations.
      </p>
      <p>
        Equivalently, define the <em>prime-coordinate map</em>
      </p>
      <p style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
        \[\varphi: D\setminus\{0\} \to \bigoplus_{p\in P}\mathbb{Z},
          \quad \varphi(x) = (v_p(x))_{p\in P}.\]
      </p>
      <p>
        The valuations satisfy for all \(x,y\in D\setminus\{0\},\,p\in P\):
      </p>
      <ol>
        <li><em>Multiplicativity:</em> \(v_p(xy)=v_p(x)+v_p(y)\).</li>
        <li><em>Triangle inequality:</em> \(v_p(x+y)\ge\min\{v_p(x),v_p(y)\}\).</li>
        <li><em>Power law:</em> \(v_p(x^n)=n\,v_p(x)\) for all \(n\in\mathbb{N}\).</li>
        <li><em>Normalization:</em> \(v_p(p)=1\) and \(v_q(p)=0\) for \(q\neq p\).</li>
        <li><em>Units:</em> \(v_p(u)=0\) for each \(u\in U(D)\).</li>
      </ol>
      <p>
        Hence, \(\varphi\) induces a group isomorphism
      </p>
      <p style={{ textAlign: 'center' }}>
        \[D^\times / U(D) \cong \bigoplus_{p\in P} \mathbb{Z},\]
      </p>
      <p>
        where \(D^\times = D\setminus\{0\}\) is the multiplicative monoid.
        This canonical factorization furnishes the algebraic backbone for the
        Prime Framework's coordinate, geometric, and spectral structures.
      </p>
    </SubsectionTemplate>
  )
}
